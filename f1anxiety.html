ANXIETY DISORDER

Therapist: Dan Aldrin Dela Cruz

Anxiety disorders are a common mental health condition characterized by intense feelings of fear, worry, and apprehension. It goes beyond the normal experience of occasional stress and nvolve excessive and persistent feelings of anxiety or fear. While it's normal to feel anxious or worried in certain situations, such as before a big presentation or a job interview, anxiety disorders go beyond these temporary feelings of stress.

Treatment for anxiety disorders often involves a combination of therapy, such as cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT), and medication, if necessary. Additionally, lifestyle changes, stress management techniques, and support from loved ones can be beneficial in managing anxiety symptoms.

It's important to note that anxiety disorders are highly treatable, and seeking professional help can make a significant difference in managing and overcoming the challenges associated with these conditions.

"Anxiety does not empty tomorrow of its sorrows but only empties today of its strength."

– Dan Aldrin Dela Cruz, therapist.

Professionals can offer different forms of therapy, such as cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT), which has shown significant effectiveness in treating anxiety disorders. They can teach you coping skills, relaxation techniques, and help you challenge and reframe negative thoughts and behaviors associated with anxiety. Dealing with anxiety can be challenging and isolating. Mental health professionals provide a supportive and non-judgmental environment where you can openly discuss your concerns, fears, and experiences. They can validate your feelings, provide guidance, and offer emotional support throughout your treatment journey. Remember, mental health professionals are trained to understand and support individuals with anxiety disorders. They can provide the guidance, tools, and resources necessary to manage your anxiety effectively and work towards recovery.
