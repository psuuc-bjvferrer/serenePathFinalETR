POST-TRAUMATIC STRESS ORDER

Therapist: Kaide Tabudlo

Manifest through intrusive thoughts, nightmares, flashbacks, and intense emotional distress. We foster open conversations, offer resources, and encourage seeking professional help to address the impact of anxiety disorders on mental health.

Anxiety disorders can manifest in various ways, and individuals may experience intrusive thoughts, nightmares, flashbacks, and intense emotional distress as part of their symptoms. Intrusive thoughts are unwanted, repetitive, and distressing thoughts that can be difficult to control or dismiss. Nightmares and flashbacks can involve vivid and distressing recollections of past traumatic events, contributing to heightened anxiety and fear. Overall, fostering open conversations, offering resources, and emphasizing the importance of seeking professional help are crucial in addressing the impact of anxiety disorders on mental health, promoting understanding, and facilitating effective support and treatment.

"Trauma is personal. It does not disappear if it is not validated. When it is ignored or invalidated, the silent screams continue internally, heard only by the one held captive."

– Kaide Tabudlo, therapist.

Professionals, such as psychiatrists or psychologists specializing in trauma, can accurately assess and diagnose traumatic stress disorders. They have the expertise to differentiate between TSD and other mental health conditions with similar symptoms, ensuring you receive an accurate diagnosis. Trauma-focused professionals can help you establish a sense of safety and stability in your life. They can assist in developing coping mechanisms, grounding techniques, and self-care practices to enhance your overall sense of well-being. Remember, seeking professional help is a crucial step in addressing and managing traumatic stress disorder. Professionals have the knowledge, expertise, and resources to provide appropriate care, facilitate healing, and help you regain control of your life after experiencing trauma.
